Accessibility

My experience with website accessibility standards dates back to about 2013, when I attended a brownbag lunch lead by one of my co-workers at the time. He introduced us all (the audience was web developers) to what accessibility was, and provided some basic tips (color contrast, ALT IMG text) on how to make our websites more accessibile.

At my next job I made it a point to gather a lot of information on accessibility and tools to test websites. I shared my knowledge of semantic HTML markup with the website content creators and successfully (I think) nudged them into using header tags for content, not for font. I presented a case to upper management to bring in a JAWS reader so that we (the development team) could better test our website. Unfortuantely that didn't happen, but it started the conversation at that orginazation.

The bulk of my information collection was from 2018, so my resource links date from that time period. I attended monthly accessibility meetups and learned about the different types of occupations such as auditors.

For the next job, I actually enrolled in a accessibility champion training program. It went into detail (and examples) of different technicques such as skip menus, keyboard navigation versus mouse navigation, etc.
